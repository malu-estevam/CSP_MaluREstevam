Arches National Park

Sooo... what about it?

Arches Nationa Park, located in eastern Utah, displays over 2,000 beautiful, natural, delicate, arches. The red rock is a shocker to the human eye, resulting in a magnificent place for a photoshoot. We understand there are people who cannot live without their phones. Well, good news, our park is mobile friendly. We also have camping sites if you love the park so much you can't leave. One could learn so much about history and culture there, because of the ancient painting the Native Americans left behind. Arches National Park has so mcuh to offer for not only nature lovers, but history lovers as well.

Why should you go?

  1. Beautiful place for senior pictures, wedding photoshoots, sunset aesthetics.
  2. A way to learn the history of Native Americans.
  3. People travel from all over the world to go- you don't want to miss out.
  4. In case you don't love it (unlikely) it is close to the city of Moab.

Facts

An interesting fact about the arches is that it used to be underwater. This is why the arches are shaped the way they are. Another facts is that the soil is actually "alive." Living organisms like green algae, mosses, and fungi actually hold the whole park together. And finally, there was at least 11 movies filmed there. Movies like Indiana Jones and The Last Crusade, Thelma and Louise, and Hulk.
